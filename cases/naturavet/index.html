NaturaVet

Where trust begins: a full inbound strategy for a bilingual pet clinic in Amsterdam.

🐾 Context & goals

My first project during digital marketing training. A fictional case: a new vet clinic in southern Amsterdam (De Pijp / Rivierenbuurt) targeting a very specific audience — expats and first-time pet parents who feel anxious, overwhelmed, and underserved by traditional clinics.

The clinic's positioning was built on three pillars: Fear-Free Certified care, a multilingual team (English, Dutch, Spanish), and deep empathy for people who love their pets but don't always know what they're doing. The brief was to create a full inbound campaign from scratch — no budget, no brand recognition, no paid ads.

✅ Everything is connected

I didn't build a funnel — I built a network. Every asset connects to the next: blogs drive quiz completions, quizzes trigger email sequences, emails link back to blogs and booking. The strength of the strategy is in the connections, not in any single piece.

I started with competitor research using SEMrush, Similarweb, and Ubersuggest — analysing three local Amsterdam clinics (Stadion Dierenkliniek, Dierenkliniek Vondelpark, Dierenkliniek Overtoom). All three were weak on social media, emotional branding, and digital tools. That gap was exactly where NaturaVet could win.

I then ran a full SWOT analysis and built two detailed buyer personas to ground every tactical decision that followed.

👥 Who I designed for

Two core personas, built from demographic research on Amsterdam South neighbourhoods

Millennial pet parents · Ages 25–40

Emotionally driven, digitally active, want flexible booking and gentle-handled visits. Best reached via Instagram, WhatsApp, and newsletters.

First time owners · Ages 23–35

Often expats or young couples, new to Amsterdam and to pet care. Need clear guidance and reassurance. Best reached via checklists, blogs, and educational Reels.

🚀 Strategy in action

Social Media plan

A 2-month content calendar across Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook — 3–4 posts/week on IG, 2/week on TikTok, with a defined content mix: 35% educational, 30% emotional, 20% promotional, 10% interactive, 5% behind-the-scenes.

April 2025 content calendar — fully mapped across IG, TikTok, Facebook, and Email

Lead magnets

Two downloadable assets built to capture leads and build trust: a Mini Lookbook and a New Pet Starter Kit Checklist in Notion format. Shared across blogs and emails to provide genuine value before asking for anything in return.

📝 Educational blogs: content with purpose

Each blog served a specific role in the funnel — not just SEO content, but strategic touchpoints designed to build trust, capture leads, and drive action.

TOFU · Attraction + engagement

1. "First-Time Pet Owner Fails? 🐾 Learn & Avoid!"

Education + Reusable Content + Instagram Engagement

A playful, emotionally resonant Gamma blog built to double as a social media engine — designed to become an Instagram Highlight, Story, or Reel series. The final section invited users to share their "pet parent fail" stories and tag the clinic, driving interaction and brand affinity.

Gamma-built blog post — designed to work as both SEO content and Instagram content

MOFU · Education + trust

2. "New Pet Starter Kit: Your Guide to a Happy Pet"

Education + Downloadable Lead Magnet

Positioned at the Engage stage, this guide covered vaccines, behaviour, and first-vet-visit prep — acting as a friendly resource for anxious first-timers. Included a Dutch Pet Vaccination Calendar and linked to the downloadable Notion checklist. Tone: "New here? We're here to guide you."

Landing page built in Gamma — pet vaccination calendar + downloadable Notion checklist

MOFU · Lead generation

3. "Do You Need Pet Insurance in the Netherlands?"

Attraction + Lead Generation + Nurturing

Targeted top-of-funnel users searching for pet insurance in the Netherlands. Featured an interactive Typeform quiz — a soft lead-generation tool disguised as helpful content — which triggered a 4-part email sequence in HubSpot with automatic segmentation by pet type.

Insurance landing page + embedded Typeform quiz — the entry point into the full nurturing journey

◆ Interactive quiz + Email automation

The Typeform quiz wasn't just a helpful tool — it was the entry point into a relationship. Once completed, answers were automatically sent to HubSpot, where contacts were segmented by pet type (dog / cat / other) for personalised follow-up.

Full funnel connection: Gamma landing page → Typeform quiz → HubSpot segmentation → email sequence

Segmentation triggered a 4 part Soap Opera style email sequence built in HubSpot:

Email 1 — Welcome

"We're reviewing your answers, and we're here for you." Warm, no pressure.

Email 2 — Empathy

Addresses overwhelm, offers the New Pet Starter Kit as a vet-approved first step.

Email 3 — Differentiation

Introduces NaturaVet's Fear-Free philosophy, multilingual team, and emotional approach.

Email 4 — Gentle CTA

Invites to book a first visit. Supportive, no hard sell. Links to booking and WhatsApp.

HubSpot workflow — the 4 email sequence, built with delays, branches, and pet type segmentation

📱 From blog to instagram

Content wasn't siloed — every blog was designed to also live as an Instagram highlight, story series, or Reel. The "First-Time Pet Owner Fails" blog, for example, was structured specifically to be repurposed as a social content engine.

Blog CTA mapped directly to the NaturaVet Instagram profile mockup — designed in UXPilot

📸 Influencer strategy

In a city full of options, trust doesn't come from ads — it comes from stories. I designed a micro-influencer strategy focused on pet parents, foster volunteers, and animal trainers in Amsterdam, prioritising authenticity over reach (profiles with 2K–15K engaged followers).

  • Share honest stories about their pets' care journeys and introduce NaturaVet as a safe, modern clinic
  • Promote the insurance quiz, blogs, and starter kit checklist
  • Invite followers to share their own "first-time pet fails" — turning UGC into organic reach

📏 Metrics & tracking

I built a full measurement framework from scratch, linking every action to a trackable KPI — across Instagram (reach, engagement, reel views, link clicks), the website (blog visits, quiz completions), Typeform (submission rate, pet-type segmentation), and email (open rate, CTR, follow-up actions).

Tracking stack: HubSpot · Meta Business Suite · GA4 · SEMrush · Metricool · Hotjar · Excel

🧠 Key learnings

NaturaVet was my first project and it gave me a foundation I still build on. The biggest shift in my thinking was understanding that inbound isn't a funnel — it's a network. No single asset works alone; each one only makes sense as part of a connected system.

  • Cross-channel thinking: Every asset links to the next. Blog → Quiz → Email → Booking → Review → UGC → back to blog.
  • Empathy as strategy: The clinic's bilingual, Fear-Free positioning wasn't just a brand choice — it was a market gap backed by competitor research and demographic data.
  • Consistency builds trust: Using the same tone across Canva, HubSpot, and Gamma made every touchpoint feel like it came from the same caring voice.

Though simulated, the rigour of building a full-funnel strategy with real tools — SEMrush, HubSpot, Typeform, Gamma, GA4 — gave me a concrete framework for approaching any future campaign.
