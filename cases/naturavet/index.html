NaturaVet

Where trust begins: a full inbound strategy for a bilingual pet clinic in Amsterdam.

🐾 Context & goals

My first project during digital marketing training. A fictional case: a new vet clinic in southern Amsterdam (De Pijp / Rivierenbuurt) for a very specific audience — expats and first-time pet parents who feel anxious, overwhelmed, and let down by traditional clinics.

The positioning was built on three things: Fear-Free Certified care, a multilingual team (English, Dutch, Spanish), and genuine empathy for people who love their pets but are figuring it all out. The brief was to build a full inbound campaign from scratch — no budget, no brand recognition, no paid ads.

✅ Everything connects

I built a network, not a funnel. Every asset links to the next: blogs drive quiz completions, quizzes trigger email sequences, emails lead back to blogs and to booking. The strategy only works because the pieces talk to each other.

I started with competitor research using SEMrush, Similarweb, and Ubersuggest — I looked at three local Amsterdam clinics (Stadion Dierenkliniek, Dierenkliniek Vondelpark, Dierenkliniek Overtoom). All three were weak on social media, emotional branding, and digital tools. That gap was exactly where NaturaVet could win.

From there: full SWOT analysis, two detailed buyer personas, and every tactical decision grounded in those.

👥 Who I designed for?

Two core personas, built from demographic research on Amsterdam South neighbourhoods.

Millennial pet parents · Ages 25–40

Emotionally driven, digitally active, want flexible booking and gentle-handled visits. Best reached via Instagram, WhatsApp, and newsletters.

First-time owners · Ages 23–35

Often expats or young couples, new to Amsterdam and to pet care. Need clear guidance and reassurance. Best reached via checklists, blogs, and educational Reels.

🚀 The strategy in action

Social media plan

A 2-month content calendar across Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook — 3–4 posts/week on IG, 2/week on TikTok, with a defined content mix: 35% educational, 30% emotional, 20% promotional, 10% interactive, 5% behind-the-scenes.

April 2025 content calendar — fully mapped across IG, TikTok, Facebook, and Email

Lead magnets

Two downloadable assets built to capture leads and earn trust before asking for anything: a Mini Lookbook and a New Pet Starter Kit Checklist in Notion format. Shared across blogs and emails.

📝 Educational blogs: content with a job to do

Each blog had a specific role in the funnel — not just SEO content, but a strategic touchpoint designed to build trust, capture leads, and push toward action.

TOFU — Attraction + engagement

"First-Time Pet Owner Fails? 🐾 Learn & Avoid!"

A playful Gamma blog designed to double as a social media engine — structured to become an Instagram Highlight, Story, or Reel series. The last section invited users to share their "pet parent fail" stories and tag the clinic, which drove engagement and real brand affinity.

Gamma blog — built to work as SEO content and repurposed Instagram content simultaneously

MOFU — Education + trust

"New Pet Starter Kit: Your Guide to a Happy Pet"

This one sat at the Engage stage and covered vaccines, behaviour, and first-vet-visit prep — a friendly resource for anxious first-timers. It included a Dutch Pet Vaccination Calendar and linked to the downloadable Notion checklist. Tone: "New here? We're here to guide you."

Gamma landing page — Dutch Pet Vaccination Calendar + downloadable Notion checklist

MOFU — Lead generation

"Do You Need Pet Insurance in the Netherlands?"

Targeted top-of-funnel users searching for pet insurance. Featured an embedded Typeform quiz — a soft lead-generation tool that felt like helpful content — which triggered a 4-part email sequence in HubSpot with automatic segmentation by pet type.

Insurance landing page + embedded Typeform quiz — entry point into the full nurturing journey

◆ Interactive quiz + email automation

The Typeform quiz was the entry point into a real relationship. Once completed, answers went straight to HubSpot, where contacts were segmented by pet type (dog / cat / other) for personalised follow-up.

Full funnel: Gamma landing page → Typeform quiz → HubSpot segmentation → email sequence

That segmentation triggered a 4-part Soap Opera-style email sequence:

Email 1 — Welcome

"We're reviewing your answers, and we're here for you." Warm, no pressure.

Email 2 — Empathy

Addresses the overwhelm, offers the New Pet Starter Kit as a vet-approved first step.

Email 3 — Differentiation

Introduces NaturaVet's Fear-Free philosophy, multilingual team, and real approach to pet care.

Email 4 — Gentle CTA

Invitation to book a first visit. Supportive, no hard sell. Links to booking and WhatsApp.

HubSpot workflow — 4-email sequence with delays, branches, and pet-type segmentation

📱 From the blog to Instagram

Content was never siloed. Every blog was designed to also live as an Instagram highlight, story series, or Reel. The "First-Time Pet Owner Fails" blog, for example, was structured specifically to be repurposed as a social content engine — the CTA mapped directly to the NaturaVet Instagram profile.

Blog CTA mapped directly to the NaturaVet Instagram profile mockup — designed in UXPilot

📸 Influencer strategy

In a city full of options, trust comes from stories, not ads. I designed a micro-influencer strategy focused on pet parents, foster volunteers, and animal trainers in Amsterdam — authenticity over reach (profiles with 2K–15K engaged followers).

The brief for collaborators:

  • Share honest stories about their pets' care journeys and introduce NaturaVet as a safe, modern clinic
  • Promote the insurance quiz, blogs, and starter kit checklist
  • Invite their followers to share their own "first-time pet fails" — turning UGC into organic reach

📏 Metrics & tracking

Full measurement framework built from scratch, linking every action to a trackable KPI across Instagram (reach, engagement, reel views, link clicks), the website (blog visits, quiz completions), Typeform (submission rate, pet-type segmentation), and email (open rate, CTR, follow-up actions).

Tracking stack: HubSpot · Meta Business Suite · GA4 · SEMrush · Metricool · Hotjar · Excel

🧠 Key learnings

NaturaVet was my first project and it gave me a foundation I still build on. The biggest shift was understanding that inbound strategy is a network — no single asset works alone. Each one only makes sense as part of a connected system.

A few things that stuck:

  • Cross-channel thinking: Every asset links to the next. Blog → Quiz → Email → Booking → Review → UGC → back to blog.
  • Empathy as a strategic choice: The clinic's bilingual, Fear-Free positioning wasn't branding for the sake of it — it came from competitor research and real demographic data. There was a clear gap, and we built into it.
  • Consistency earns trust: Using the same tone across Canva, HubSpot, and Gamma made every touchpoint feel like it came from the same place.

And the practical side: working with real tools — SEMrush, HubSpot, Typeform, Gamma, GA4 — even on a fictional case gives you a concrete framework for the real thing. That part wasn't simulated.
