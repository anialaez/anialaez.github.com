NaturaVet

Case Study

Where trust begins: an inbound journey for pet care in Amsterdam.

🐾 Context & Goals

This was my first project during my digital marketing training. We worked with a fictional case: a new veterinary clinic in southern Amsterdam focused on emotional, multilingual, and fear-free care. The goal was to create a full inbound marketing campaign from scratch to position the clinic among expats and new pet parents — with no existing brand recognition or paid campaigns.

I wanted to emotionally connect with people who love their pets deeply but feel anxious about how to care for them properly. So I designed an inbound strategy tailored to their needs and emotions, providing useful, kind, and calming content. Everything was built around education, empathy, and long-term trust.

"New here? We're here to guide you."

✅ Everything is Connected

Each piece of content leads to the next. From blogs to lead magnets, Instagram reels to Typeform quizzes, everything is designed to engage our audience across channels and gently guide them toward booking their first visit.

This isn't a funnel — it's a network. Its strength lies in the connections.

🚀 Strategy in Action

Market & SEO Research

I conducted a full SWOT analysis, explored competitors using SEMrush, Similarweb, and Ubersuggest, and defined realistic positioning for a small local clinic. I then built three buyer personas and a complete inbound strategy for the brand launch.

Social Media Plan

I designed a 3-month content plan (Excel) for Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook, built around trust signals, pet care tips, emotional storytelling, and clinic highlights. Content was designed in Canva and Gamma, with video content created in VideoAI and InvideoAI.

Lead Magnets

I created two downloadable, useful, and visually appealing lead magnets: a Mini Lookbook and a Starter Kit Checklist (Notion format). These were shared across blogs and email sequences to provide value and capture leads.

📝 Educational Blogs: Content with Purpose

Content wasn't just created to exist — it was designed to work. Each blog post served a unique purpose within the inbound funnel, contributing to both SEO visibility and customer trust.

1. "Do You Need Pet Insurance in the Netherlands?"

Attraction + Lead Generation + Nurturing

Targeted top-of-the-funnel users searching for pet insurance in the Netherlands. It featured an interactive Typeform quiz — a soft lead-generation tool disguised as helpful content. Upon completion, responses triggered a 4-part Soap Opera-style email sequence in HubSpot, with automatic segmentation by pet type (dog, cat, other).

This blog was more than just content — it was the entry point into a fully automated nurturing journey.

2. "New Pet Starter Kit: Your Guide to a Happy Pet"

Education + Trust + Downloadable Lead Magnet

Positioned at the Engage stage, this blog aimed to build trust. With clear advice on vaccines, behaviour, and preparation tips for a first vet visit, it functioned as a friendly guide for nervous first-timers.

It introduced a Notion-based downloadable checklist, reinforcing our image as a kind, practical clinic. The tone was intentionally soft: "New here? We're here to guide you."

3. "First-Time Pet Owner Fails? 🐾 Learn & Avoid!"

Education + Reusable Content + Instagram Engagement

This playful, emotionally resonant piece lived at the top of the funnel and doubled as a social media engine. Created as an engaging Gamma slide blog, it was built to become an Instagram Highlight, Story, or Reel series. Each slide tackled a common mistake, and the final one invited users to share their funniest "pet parent fail" stories and tag the clinic — boosting interaction and brand affinity.

◆ Interactive Quiz + Email Sequence

I designed an interactive Typeform quiz to help new pet owners decide whether pet insurance made sense for their pet. But this quiz wasn't just a helpful tool — it was the starting point of a relationship.

Once completed, user answers were automatically sent to HubSpot, where contacts were segmented by pet type for tailored follow-up. This triggered a 4-part Soap Opera-style email sequence:

Email 1

Warm welcome and reassurance — "We're reviewing your answers, and we're here for you."

Email 2

Emotional storytelling — addressing overwhelm and offering the New Pet Starter Kit as a clear, vet-approved first step.

Email 3

Brand differentiation — introducing NaturaVet's Fear-Free philosophy, multilingual team, and emotional care.

Email 4

Gentle urgency — inviting the user to book a first visit, while reinforcing the supportive, no-pressure tone.

Every email included links to relevant blogs, social channels, and a soft CTA — creating a loop of nurturing content that built trust, value, and emotional safety.

📸 Influencer Campaign: Trust Through Shared Stories

In a city full of options, trust doesn't come from ads — it comes from stories. I created a micro-influencer strategy focused on pet parents, foster volunteers, and trainers in Amsterdam and across the Netherlands.

I identified profiles with 2K–15K engaged followers, prioritising authenticity over reach. These influencers would:

  • Share honest, emotional stories about their pets' care journeys
  • Introduce NaturaVet as a safe, modern, and multilingual clinic
  • Promote key resources like educational blogs, the insurance quiz, and the starter kit checklist
  • Encourage interaction, inviting followers to take the quiz or share their own first-time pet fails

📏 Metrics & Tracking Plan

I developed a full metrics plan from the ground up, linking each marketing action to tangible, trackable results. KPIs defined across platforms:

  • Instagram: Reach, engagement rate, story and reel views, link clicks to website
  • Website: Visits to blog posts and landing pages, quiz completions
  • Typeform: Quiz submission rate, new contacts segmented by pet type
  • Email: Open rates, CTR, follow-up actions
  • Clinic goals: Number of first-time appointments booked

Tracking through: HubSpot, Meta Business Suite, GA4, Excel.

🧠 Key Learnings

NaturaVet taught me how to create an inbound campaign with real emotional depth and strategic alignment. I learned to map out each touchpoint — from a quiz to a blog to an email — as part of a connected journey.

Some of my most valuable lessons:

  • Cross-channel thinking: Every asset links to the next step. No action stands alone — they all serve a shared purpose.
  • Content that converts gently: Instead of pushing offers, I crafted a path based on empathy, guidance, and subtle cues to action.
  • Message consistency: Using the same reassuring voice across Canva, HubSpot, and Gamma — making people feel safe from the very first click.

This was my first project, and though simulated, it gave me a strong foundation in designing campaigns that feel human, are trackable, and are built for impact — not vanity.
