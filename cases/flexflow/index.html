FlexFlow

Case Study

What if wellness could adapt to your daily chaos?

Context & Kick-off

FlexFlow is a digital wellness platform that delivers live and on-demand yoga and pilates classes tailored for modern lifestyles. The brief was clear: reach people who need it most but feel like fitness isn't made for them.

The goal was to achieve 100 free trial sign-ups and 1,200 website visits within 30 days, using a €3,000 budget split across Google Ads and Instagram influencer partnerships. This was a bootcamp project at Ironhack, but we treated it as a real brief from the start.

The Strategy

Most wellness platforms talk to everyone the same way. FlexFlow's campaign was built around the opposite idea: three very different people, with three very different reasons to start yoga, each met with messaging that spoke directly to their emotional reality.

The strategy focused on three pillars:

  1. Persona-led storytelling: Each persona had their own channel mix, ad copy, keywords, and funnel entry point.
  2. Non-linear funnel: Sara converted from emotional TOFU Reels; Sophie went from awareness to conversion almost instantly; David started mid-funnel with high search intent.
  3. Channel synergy: Instagram told the story. Google closed the deal. Each channel had a defined role — not a copy-paste of the same message across platforms.

The Three Personas

Each persona drove a different channel mix, ad copy, keywords, and funnel entry point.

Sara

The Anxious Beginner

Intimidated by fitness spaces. Needs emotional safety and zero pressure to get started.

Keywords

beginner yoga at home · gentle yoga · yoga for inflexible people

Ad message

"Zero-pressure yoga. Step-by-step. Try free."

Enters via emotional TOFU Reels

Sophie

The Overwhelmed Mom

Feels invisible in traditional fitness spaces. Craves calm and structure in a chaotic routine.

Keywords

quiet yoga for moms · bedtime yoga · stress relief yoga

Ad message

"Quiet yoga made for busy moms – Try free for 7 days."

Awareness to conversion almost instantly

David

The Burned-Out Professional

No time, chronic back pain. Wants fast, practical relief — not inspiration.

Keywords

yoga for back pain · 10 min yoga · desk worker stretch

Ad message

"Back pain? Burnout? Reset in 10 minutes."

Starts mid-funnel with high search intent

Technical Setup & Tracking

Every touchpoint was connected and measurable from day one. GTM, GA4, HubSpot and UTM parameters were set up before the first ad went live — attribution built into the structure, not added as an afterthought.

Landing Pages & Wireframes

Each persona had a dedicated landing page experience — copy, visuals and CTA adapted to their specific motivation and funnel stage. The final landing page was designed for a calm, guided UI experience with a clear trial CTA.

Ad Campaigns

Google Ads

Search, Brand and Remarketing campaigns built around high-intent keywords for each persona. Ad copy was written to match the emotional state of the searcher — not just the query.

YouTube Ads

Short video ads tailored to each persona — aspirational tone for David, nap-time calm for Sophie, zero-pressure safety for Sara.

Media Plan & Budget

Total monthly budget: €3,000 split across Google Ads, Instagram influencer partnerships and Google Display retargeting. Every euro was tracked against a specific persona and funnel stage.

Performance Dashboard

All campaign data was visualised in a Looker Studio dashboard, connected to GA4 and Google Ads. The dashboard tracked performance by persona, channel and funnel stage in real time.

Key Learnings

This project taught me that the channel is rarely the problem — the brief is. When you understand who you're talking to at an emotional level, the messaging, the format and the funnel almost write themselves.

  • Designing for a non-linear funnel forces you to think from the customer's perspective, not from the campaign structure
  • Emotional resonance in ad copy outperforms urgency tactics — especially in wellness
  • Channel synergy only works when each channel has a clear, distinct role
  • Setting up tracking before launch — not after — changes the quality of every decision you make during optimisation
